  • About our team

    Buying art made easy: ARTGALLERY is an online gallery for contemporary art that allows collectors and art lovers alike to buy works of art in complete security from nationally recognized artists. From abstract canvases, figurative paintings, drawings or even street art and graffiti, we offer an international selection of work in a variety of styles and techniques. ARTGALLERY is also helping emerging artists from around the globe to sell their works to art lovers.

    Praphul

    Praphul is an established Indian artist with national exposure. His incredibly detailed acrylic on canvas paintings depict fantastical scenes full of wonder and imagination. He is particularly captivated by the image of hot air balloons which appear throughout his oeuvre. his works are stunning, rich canvases that are a testament to his aesthetic as well as technical accomplishments.

    Piyush

    Piyush is a Indian painter and drawer with a strong focus in oil, watercolour and gouache. He completed his formal education in art, graduating from The Indian College of Art and Technology with a BA Hons Degree Fine Art Painting. As members of his family have worked in artisan trades, Piyush utilises certain competencies in his work.

    Khushi

    Khushi is a passionate Indian painter whose work centers on a female approach and a female muse. Exploring mindfulness and meditation, beauty and timelessness, she creates emotionally-charged paintings, drawings and prints that capture a sense of being.

    Paras

    Paras is an experienced painter from India whose work has been featured in solo and group shows internationally. With a background in design, abhishek developed his artistic style creating cutting edge abstract works with vibrant and exciting sculptural elements. Working in mixed media, he uses techniques of layering, distressing and blending to build unique surfaces with depth.

    Yash

    Yash is a Indian artist whose distinctive style finds its inspiration in the Surrealist movement. Appropriating the technique of automatic drawing, Yash works are explorations into the personal, and sometimes collective, unconscious. Working with these preliminary scribbles, he gives life to forms and figures, that rather than being concrete, instead allow her viewers to create and imagine their own narratives.
